# Theorem: Rank-1 Transfer Matrix for Corrsum (L1.2 — Spectral Proof)

## Setup

Let $p$ be a prime dividing $d(k) = 2^S - 3^k$ with $p > 3$.  
Let $G = \langle 2, 3 \rangle \subset (\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$ be the subgroup generated by 2 and 3.  
Let $\omega = e^{2\pi i/p}$ be a primitive $p$-th root of unity.

For each additive character $\chi_a : x \mapsto \omega^{ax}$ with $a \in \{0, 1, \ldots, p-1\}$, define the **transfer matrix** $M_a$ on the state space $G$ by:

$$M_a[Q', Q] = \sum_{\substack{g = 1 \\ 3 \cdot 2^g \cdot Q \equiv Q' \pmod{p}}}^{\mathrm{ord}_p(2)} \omega^{a \cdot 2^g \cdot Q}$$

This matrix encodes the corrsum automaton: state $Q \in G$ tracks the "running multiplicative factor" $3^m \cdot 2^E \bmod p$, and each gap $g \geq 1$ induces the transition $Q \mapsto 3 \cdot 2^g \cdot Q$ with phase $\omega^{a \cdot 2^g \cdot Q}$.

## Main Theorem

**Theorem (Rank-1 Transfer Matrix).** Under the above setup:

1. The set of multipliers $\{3 \cdot 2^g \bmod p : g = 1, \ldots, \mathrm{ord}_p(2)\}$ is a permutation of $G$.

2. The matrix $M_a$ has the explicit form:
$$M_a[Q', Q] = \omega^{a \cdot 3^{-1} \cdot Q'} \quad \text{for all } Q, Q' \in G$$
In particular, $M_a$ is **independent of $Q$** (every column is identical).

3. Therefore $\mathrm{rank}(M_a) = 1$ for all $a \neq 0$, with unique eigenvalue:
$$\lambda_a = \sum_{Q \in G} \omega^{a \cdot 3^{-1} \cdot Q}$$

4. The magnitude satisfies:
   - (a) If $G = (\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$: $|\lambda_a| = 1$ for all $a \neq 0$ (Ramanujan sum).
   - (b) If $[(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^* : G] = 2$ and $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$: $|\lambda_a| = \sqrt{(p+1)/4}$ for all $a \neq 0$ (quadratic Gauss sum).
   - (c) In general: $|\lambda_a| \leq \sqrt{p}$ (Weil bound).

## Proof sketch

**Step 1 (Permutation property).** Since $p \mid d = 2^S - 3^k$, we have $2^S \equiv 3^k \pmod{p}$, so 2 and 3 are both invertible mod $p$ and $G = \langle 2, 3 \rangle$. The map $g \mapsto 3 \cdot 2^g \bmod p$ sends $\{1, \ldots, \mathrm{ord}_p(2)\}$ bijectively onto $3 \cdot \langle 2 \rangle = \langle 2, 3 \rangle = G$.

**Step 2 (Column independence).** For a pair $(Q, Q') \in G \times G$, the unique gap $g$ sending $Q$ to $Q'$ satisfies $2^g \equiv Q'/(3Q) \pmod{p}$, so the phase is $\omega^{a \cdot Q'/(3Q) \cdot Q} = \omega^{a \cdot Q'/3}$, independent of $Q$.

**Step 3 (Gauss sum evaluation).**
$$\lambda_a = \sum_{Q \in G} \omega^{a \cdot 3^{-1} \cdot Q}$$

If $G = (\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$, this is $\sum_{Q=1}^{p-1} \omega^{bQ} = -1$ where $b = a \cdot 3^{-1} \not\equiv 0$.

If $[G : (\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*] = 2$, decompose using the Legendre symbol:
$$\lambda_a = \frac{1}{2}\left(-1 + \left(\frac{b}{p}\right) \cdot g(\chi_2)\right)$$
where $g(\chi_2)$ is the quadratic Gauss sum with $|g(\chi_2)| = \sqrt{p}$ and $g(\chi_2) = i\sqrt{p}$ for $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$. Then $|\lambda_a| = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1 + p} = \sqrt{(p+1)/4}$. $\square$

## Consequence: L1.2 with optimal constants

The character sum for corrsum mod $p$ satisfies:
$$S_p(a) = \sum_{\sigma \in \mathrm{Comp}(S, k)} \omega^{a \cdot \mathrm{corrsum}(\sigma)} = \mathbf{v}_0^T \cdot M_a^{k-1} \cdot \mathbf{v}_f$$

Since $M_a$ has rank 1, $M_a^{k-1} = \lambda_a^{k-1} \cdot \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{w}^T$ and:
$$|S_p(a)| \leq |\lambda_a|^{k-1} \cdot C$$

By Fourier inversion:
$$N_p(r) = \frac{1}{p} \sum_{a=0}^{p-1} \omega^{-ar} \cdot S_p(a) = \frac{N}{p} + \frac{1}{p} \sum_{a=1}^{p-1} \omega^{-ar} \cdot S_p(a)$$

Therefore:
$$\left|\frac{N_p(r)}{N} - \frac{1}{p}\right| \leq \frac{p-1}{p} \cdot \frac{|\lambda_{\max}|^{k-1}}{|G|^{k-1}} \cdot C$$

This is **exponentially small** in $k$ since $|\lambda_a|/|G| < 1$ for all tested primes.
